CANCER 101

What is Cancer?

Cancer is a genetic disease in which cells divide uncontrollably and become increasingly abnormal. The disease can be initiated from almost anywhere in the body.

Normal cells stop dividing and die after they become old or damaged, but cancer cells do not follow these orders and in turn, become invasive. Cancer cells can harm healthy cells and weaken the immune system.

As an increasing amount of cancer cells form, they can form lumps of tissue called tumours. Tumors can be benign or malignant. Benign tumours do not spread whereas malignant (cancerous) tumours do. The process of spreading into other tissues is called metastasis.

Causes

Cancer is caused from mutations in DNA. These mutations can be a result of long-term exposure to carcinogens either from the environment (ex. air pollution, radiation, etc.) or poor lifestyle choices (ex.smoking, alcoholism, obesity ,etc.). It can also be hereditary.
