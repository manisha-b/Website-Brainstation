Research

Research is our second-best weapon against cancer. From research we now have a better understanding of the 200+ diseases called cancer. Improved research and prevention stragies are helping canadians live longer. Thanks to the research, the 5 year survival rate has now been improved to 60% from 25% in the 1940s. With donations, the Canadian Cancer Society can invest more money in research. The ultimate goal is to make cancer manageable.

Some researches doubt there will ever be an universal cure for cancer. Though, researches have found a great deal of information about cancer in the last few decades and will continue to do so. Here is a list of a few of the milestones that have been reached.

  1. Colbalt therapy first used to treat tumours (1950)
  2. Improving chemotherapy and radiation treatments (1960)
  3. Why cancer cells become resistant (1970)
  4. Bone marrow transplatation (1980)

Why Donate?

Investing in the best research has led to tremendous progress against cancer. We know more about what causes cancer, how it develops, how best to treat it and how we can improve the quality of life of people living with cancer. Today, over 60% of Canadians diagnosed with cancer will survive at least 5 years after their diagnosis. In the 1940s, survival was about 25%.
-Canadian Cancer Society

Thanks to Canadians like you, the Society has channeled over $1.3 billion to cancer research since the 1940s. In that time, Canadians’ 5-year survival rate has increased from 25% to more than 60%.
-Canadian Cancer Society

About half of cancers can be prevented, and screening for certain cancers can result in early detection, when the disease is most treatable. With your support, we’re getting these life-changing messages to more Canadians. We’re also funding research in cancer prevention to understand all of its causes and testing new ways for Canadians to reduce their risk of developing cancer.
-Canadian Cancer Society

Our Partner

Established by the Saskatchewan Medical Association in 1938, the Canadian Cancer Society was the first cancer committee ever formed in Canada. Today, the volunteer run organization is known as the most charitable funder of cancer research in the nation! From funding internationally recognized breakthroughs to raising public awareness and providing support to Canadians living with cancer, they are leading the fight against cancer and making a real difference.
